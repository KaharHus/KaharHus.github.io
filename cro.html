B2C · Sustainable Fashion

The Little Loop

Converting Dragons' Den traffic into subscribers

UX Audit Usability Testing

The Problem

The Little Loop anticipated a major traffic spike following their appearance on Dragons' Den. With a narrow window to capitalise on increased visibility, they needed to understand why users were abandoning the signup and subscription journey — and what changes would convert that traffic into subscribers.

What I Did

Conducted moderated in-depth interviews and usability testing across key pages and tasks. Focused on understanding why visitors came, what they tried to do, and where they dropped off — with a specific lens on the subscription flow.

Key Findings & Recommendations

Trust signals — Users needed reassurance. Recommended prioritising the "featured in" carousel, surfacing brand partnerships earlier, and integrating Trustpilot reviews.

Cognitive overload — Homepage was overwhelming. Recommended restructuring the nav and reordering content to surface how it works, pricing, and value for money before anything else.

Value messaging — Recommended reframing around a hypothetical parent persona with multiple spending scenarios to make it personal and relatable.

Feature discovery — Key subscription features weren't visible early enough. Recommended longform content and clearer plan labels in the "pick a plan" section.

Stock anxiety — Users disengaged when desired items showed as out of stock. Recommended reassurance messaging and a "notify me when available" feature.

Mobile experience — Site wasn't optimised for small screens. Recommended a content-first approach, removing visual clutter, and ensuring full responsiveness throughout.

Outcome

Delivered a prioritised set of conversion recommendations ahead of the Dragons' Den broadcast. Findings gave the team a clear brief to redesign key journey stages — with a strong hypothesis that clearly communicating the value proposition onsite would directly increase subscription conversion.

B2C · Massage Therapy

Optimal Massage

Building a stronger local presence and better website

Website Design Local SEO

The Problem

A massage therapist with strong client reviews and a loyal base, but an inconsistent online presence holding back growth. The website was costly, outdated, and not optimised for local search — limiting new client acquisition and making it harder for people to find and trust the business online.

What I Did

Conducted a full audit of the Google Business profile and existing website. Designed and built a newer, cheaper-to-run website with improved branding, imagery, and UX. Optimised for local SEO — including crafting a focused business description, selecting the right categories, and positioning the therapist's multilingual offer (English, Italian, Spanish) as a competitive differentiator.

Key Improvements

Brand consistency — Unified the business name and messaging across all platforms to avoid confusion and build recognition.

Google Business optimisation — Rewrote the business description with relevant keywords, added primary and secondary service categories, and highlighted multilingual capabilities to broaden reach.

Website redesign — Built a cleaner, faster site with better imagery and a clear service hierarchy — making it easier for visitors to understand the offer and book.

Local SEO — Optimised for searches in both Mallorca and London, with location-specific language and a strong call-to-action throughout.

Outcome

Increased inbound enquiries and faster response from prospective clients — contributing to a measurable uplift in monthly revenue.

B2C · Fashion E-Commerce

Bangladesh 1971

Brand strategy and growth roadmap for a culturally rooted fashion label

Brand Strategy Shopify Workshop Facilitation

The Problem

A growing fashion e-commerce brand with strong community roots, but the founder lacked the capacity and strategic know-how to scale. The brand needed clearer positioning, a stronger digital presence, and a structured roadmap to grow sustainably.

What I Did

Ran discovery workshops to define the brand's identity, audience, values, and goals. Analysed Shopify data to identify drop-off points and redesign key aspects of the website and purchase journey. Built a strategic roadmap across five areas: business development, branding, community engagement, marketing & UX, and supply chain.

Areas of Work

Business development — Defined growth targets, mapped what scaling would require, and identified short and long-term milestones.

Branding — Developed brand guidelines covering tone, visuals, and storytelling. Aligned all touchpoints — website, social, and packaging — under a single identity.

Community engagement — Planned pop-ups, cultural events, and partnerships to connect the brand with diaspora and local communities offline.

Marketing & UX — Reviewed the Shopify site experience, refreshed Instagram content strategy, and created a 3-month content plan aligned to brand values.

Supply chain — Built a supplier tracker, evaluated partners against quality and reliability criteria, and strengthened relationships with suppliers in Bangladesh.

Outcome

A more compelling and consistent social media presence, increased website traffic, a clearer brand identity, and a stronger supply chain foundation — giving the founder a clear path to scale.
