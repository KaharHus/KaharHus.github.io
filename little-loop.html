The Little Loop

Converting Dragons' Den traffic into subscribers

The Problem

The Little Loop were about to appear on Dragons' Den. They had a narrow window to convert a spike in traffic into subscribers. The problem was clear: users were dropping out of the signup and subscription journey, and the team needed to know why and what to fix before the broadcast.

What I Did

I ran moderated usability testing across the site, focusing on the subscription flow and signup journey. I wanted to understand why visitors arrived, what they were trying to do, and where they gave up. From there, I redesigned key flows and pages and tested them with users to validate the changes.

Key Findings

Trust Signals

Users needed trust signals before committing. The "featured in" carousel, brand partnerships, and Trustpilot reviews were either absent or buried.

Cognitive Overload

The homepage created cognitive overload. How it works, pricing, and value messaging needed to come first.

Stock Anxiety

Out-of-stock items caused users to disengage. There was no reassurance messaging or restock notification to keep them in the funnel.

Mobile Experience

The mobile experience was broken. Visual clutter and missing responsiveness created drop-off across the subscription journey.

Outcome

Implemented design and content changes across the subscription flow, and key landing pages. Trust signals, value messaging, and mobile experience were all updated before the Dragons' Den broadcast.

The changes drove a measurable increase in enquiries, subscription conversions, and purchases. The resulting data gave the founder concrete evidence of commercial traction, which contributed directly to securing thousands of pounds in investment on Dragons' Den.
