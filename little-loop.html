The Little Loop

Converting Dragons' Den traffic into subscribers

The Problem

The Little Loop anticipated a major traffic spike following their appearance on Dragons' Den. With a narrow window to capitalise on increased visibility, they needed to understand why users were abandoning the signup and subscription journey — and what changes would convert that traffic into subscribers.

What I Did

Conducted moderated in-depth interviews and usability testing across key pages and tasks. Focused on understanding why visitors came, what they tried to do, and where they dropped off — with a specific lens on the subscription flow and signup journey.

Key Findings & Recommendations

Trust signals

Users needed reassurance before committing. Recommended prioritising the "featured in" carousel, surfacing brand partnerships earlier, and integrating Trustpilot reviews prominently.

Cognitive overload

Homepage was overwhelming. Recommended restructuring the nav and reordering content to surface how it works, pricing, and value for money before anything else.

Value messaging

"Is renting good value?" content wasn't landing. Recommended reframing around a hypothetical parent persona with multiple spending scenarios to make the comparison personal and relatable.

Feature discovery

Key subscription features weren't visible early enough. Recommended longform content and clearer plan labels in the "pick a plan" section to help users understand what they're signing up for.

Stock anxiety

Users disengaged when desired items showed as out of stock. Recommended reassurance messaging and a "notify me when available" feature to keep users in the funnel.

Mobile experience

Site wasn't optimised for small screens. Recommended a content-first approach, removing visual clutter, and ensuring full responsiveness throughout the subscription journey.

Outcome

Delivered a prioritised set of conversion recommendations ahead of the Dragons' Den broadcast. Findings gave the team a clear brief to redesign key journey stages — with a strong hypothesis that clearly communicating the value proposition onsite would directly increase subscription conversion.
